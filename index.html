About Me

My mom was a computer programmer in the 70s for the state of Missouri. She used punch cards to run the payroll for the state and broke down barriers to women in tech even back then. She made sure that we always had a computer in the home for us to learn those skills and understand how they work. I grew up learning how to do basic coding when I was 10 and did my first big programming project in high school on an Apple 2e. :)


I taught computer fundamentals, MS Office and database fundamentals at a community college in my early 20s and then decided that people and how they worked was more interesting. So I finished my undergrad and graduate degrees in Marriage and Family Therapy. I owned a private therapy practice since graduate school where I focused on treating couples and families, and individuals dealing with trauma.


As I have transitioned back into the tech world, I have become enamored with blockchain and NFTs. The possibilities for using these in the current world and future are so very exciting to me both from a business and creative point of view.


In my personal life, I love playing pickleball, crafting, trying out new restaurants with friends, and exploring National Parks.
